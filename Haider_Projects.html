Elevator Project 5/18/16 - Principles of Engineering

The elevator project required my group to create an elevator using RobotC programming and VEX parts. Our idea consisted of a chain and sprocket system and a platform attached to it. The chain and sprocket have 2 gears that are on their own axle. Each axle is powered by their own motor too. A platform that represents the elevator will be screwed to the chain and sprocket and all of this will be supported by different VEX parts. Three limit switches are for calling the elevator to the corresponding floor and 3 more bump switches are for going to each floor after the call. Both motors will be programmed to move simultaneously at the same speed so the elevator moves to whatever floor that’s inputted.

Entrepreneurship Project 5/14/16 - Principles of Engineering

The entreprenuership project allowed us to solve any problem with our own solution. The problem we addressed was dirty windows not being able to be cleaned easily. We created the Jet Spray Window Cleaner 5000 using VEX parts and RobotC programming. The jet spray uses pressurized water from the house’s plumbing system through the hose into a pvc pipe and out through the nozzle. The spigot is attached to the hose, which connects the house’s plumbing system to the device. The hose is then attached to the control valve which allows the user to control the water pressure. The nozzle is set to have a water pressure of 35 psi just to get the right amount of water and force. The water enters the pvc pipe and finally exits through multiple nozzles, which were inserted through drilled holes and connected by using pvc cement glue. The water is sprayed towards the glass at a 45 degree angle right before the windshield wiper wipes the window.

Scratch Project 9/16/16 - Computer Science Engineering

The objective of this project was to create a game or story on Scratch that contained multiple phases or stages. My partner and I decided to create a game based off the 1989 Nintendo Game Boy game Alleyway. To meet the requirement of multiple phases, we created two levels for the player to go through which increased in difficulty. To create the game on Scratch we programmed the game together and separately on our own time. One major challenge was programming the ball to bounce off the blocks and afterward, making the blocks dissapear. Once we finished the main content, I polished the game with music, extra sprites, and "Game Over" screens. My partner and I relied on our communication skills to make sure we both knew what we wanted for the final version of the game. It all worked out as we created a decent game -one of the better ones in the class- considering time constraints and little to no experience in Scratch.
